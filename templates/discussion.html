Mathematical Discussion

Introduction

Gaussian Elimination is a numerical method used to solve systems of linear equations by transforming the augmented matrix into an upper triangular form. Once in this form, the system becomes easier to solve using back substitution.

The method is widely used in engineering, physics, and computer science because it provides a systematic and reliable way to solve multiple equations with multiple unknowns.

What is Gaussian Elimination?

The goal of Gaussian Elimination is to simplify a system of equations step-by-step until it becomes triangular. Each step removes variables from lower equations, reducing the complexity of the system while preserving its solution.

Partial Pivoting

Partial pivoting improves numerical stability by swapping rows so that the largest available coefficient is used as the pivot. This reduces rounding errors and prevents division by very small or zero values.

Without pivoting, the elimination process may become unstable or fail in some cases.

Row Elimination Formula

Rⱼ = Rⱼ − mⱼᵢRᵢ

The multiplier mji is computed as the ratio of the element being eliminated to the pivot element.

Back Substitution

Once the matrix is in upper triangular form, the last equation is solved first. The solution is then substituted upward step-by-step to find all unknown variables.

Advantages

  • Avoids division by zero through pivoting
  • Improves numerical stability
  • Reduces rounding errors in computation
  • Works efficiently for large systems of equations
